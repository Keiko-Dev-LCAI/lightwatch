How LightView Works

⚡ One add-on. Any business. Any size. Zero disruption.
LightView sits on top of whatever systems you already have and adds AI monitoring and blockchain verification. It doesn't matter if you're a one-person dry cleaner or a global aerospace company — if your business moves things, tracks things, or manages things, LightView makes it smarter and verifiable.
🚗
Think about TPMS — tire pressure sensors. When your car's low-pressure light comes on, it didn't rebuild your wheels. A small sensor was added that monitors what's already there and alerts you when something is off.

LightView is that sensor for your business. We don't replace your software. We don't change how you work. We add a monitoring layer on top. When something goes wrong — or is about to — you know immediately.

The Three Layers
1
Your existing systems

What you already have — unchanged

Your factory floor systems, warehouse scanners, shipping APIs, POS software, financial records — whatever you have. LightView connects to these and reads from them. You don't replace anything. Everything you already paid for keeps working exactly as it does today. Workers with handheld scanners keep scanning. Warehouse teams keep using their software. Nothing changes for them.

2
AIVM — inside your network

AI that watches for what humans miss

Our AI model runs inside your network and monitors your data continuously. It understands rules you write in plain English — "Alert if the cooler goes above 41°F" or "Flag if a shipment sits more than 4 hours." It learns your normal patterns and catches anomalies before they become problems. Your raw data never leaves your building. Only the AI's conclusions go anywhere.

3
Lightchain — public blockchain

A timestamp no one can argue with

When something important happens — an alert fires, a scan completes, a rule changes, a daily snapshot is taken — a permanent record is written to the Lightchain blockchain. This timestamp is cryptographically sealed. It can't be edited, backdated, or deleted. Your bank, your regulator, your auditor, your insurance company — anyone can independently verify it without asking you for anything.

Why does blockchain matter?

A spreadsheet you made says whatever you want it to say. An email timestamp can be faked. Even your own internal systems can be edited after the fact — and everyone knows it.

A blockchain timestamp can't. When LightView writes a record to Lightchain, it's sealed. If your cooler failed at 12:03 PM, that record exists forever. You didn't decide to write it after the health inspector showed up. That's the difference between "we have a record" and "we have proof."
